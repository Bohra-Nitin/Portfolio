Interactive Traffic Signs Demo Instructional Design Portfolio

A scenario-based eLearning module designed to teach learners the shapes, categories, and exceptions of traffic signs using drag-and-drop, fill-in-the-blank, and gamified feedback layers.

Launch Demo

Project Overview

This demo course introduces learners to traffic sign categories through interactive practice. The design emphasizes usability, logical feedback, and scenario-based reinforcement. Learners engage with drag-and-drop activities, timed challenges, and exception layers to deepen understanding of mandatory, compulsory, and informatory signs.

Audience

Novice drivers, compliance trainees, and learners preparing for road safety certifications.

Duration

5 micro-lessons (~30 minutes) with interactive practice and final quiz.

Tools Used

Articulate Storyline, HTML5, Tailwind CSS, accessibility testing tools.

My Role

Instructional Designer, Interaction Developer, Feedback Architect.

Course Objectives

  • Identify shapes of mandatory, compulsory, and informatory signs.
  • Apply knowledge through drag-and-drop and fill-in-the-blank activities.
  • Recognize exceptions (e.g., STOP octagon, GIVE WAY inverted triangle).
  • Receive logical, scenario-based feedback for reinforcement.
  • Demonstrate mastery through a final assessment with certificate generation.

Learning Design Methodology

The demo course was designed using Backward Design and Scenario-Based Learning to ensure strong alignment between objectives, activities, and assessments.

  • Outcomes-first approach: Focus on accurate recognition of traffic signs.
  • Scenario-driven learning: Learners practice with real-world driving contexts.
  • Interactive media: Drag-and-drop, timed challenges, and layered feedback.
  • Assessment strategy: Final quiz with certificate generation.
  • Iterative improvement: Design refined through learner feedback and usability testing.

Gamified Flow Zone

To encourage action-first learning, the course integrates gamified elements that reward progress and sustain engagement. Learners experience a flow zone where each correct action unlocks immediate feedback, partial star fills, and progress bar updates.

Progress Bar & Stars

Four stars represent 20 questions. Each star fills in quarters as learners answer correctly, visually reinforcing mastery and motivating continued effort.

Feedback Layers

Gamified feedback layers (Correct, Incorrect, Times Up, Congratulations) use traffic metaphors like “Green Light” or “Detour Needed” to make learning immersive and playful.

Timed Challenges

Time-bound questions simulate real driving decisions, encouraging quick recognition of signs under pressure. This gamified mechanic builds confidence and mirrors real-world urgency.

Action-First Learning

Learners are placed directly into decision-making scenarios before theory is explained. This “action-first” approach ensures knowledge is anchored in experience, with gamified rewards reinforcing correct actions.

Feedback & Certification Workflow

  • Feedback delivered via logical layers (Correct, Incorrect, Times Up, Congratulations).
  • Exceptions layer reveals special cases for deeper learning.
  • Gamified sounds and animations (chimes, confetti) reinforce achievement.
  • Completion data validated before certificate generation.
  • Certificates automatically generated and shared with learners.
